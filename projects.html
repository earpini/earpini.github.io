On the menu is a browser game about middle-power leverage in the AI order, built on the same framework as my working paper on Brazil in the global AI order. You play a middle power converting what you hold into a seat at the table, while two AI poles try to buy your coalition partners out from under you.

Eight turns, three scenarios, 18 playable countries, five endings — including the one the paper calls the accommodation trap: the coalition that gets more effective attracts more pressure to break apart.
